# Translation Workflow Guide

Professional translation process for luxury brand content with focus on tone preservation and quality.

## Transcreation vs Translation

**Translation:** Converting text from one language to another
**Transcreation:** Recreating the emotional impact and brand essence

For luxury brands, use **transcreation** - preserve feelings, not just words.

## Luxury Brand Voice Guidelines

### English Tone Characteristics
- Refined and sophisticated
- Confident without arrogance
- Descriptive yet concise
- Emphasizes heritage, craftsmanship, exclusivity
- Uses aspirational language

### Arabic Tone Characteristics
- Formal and respectful (فصحى modern standard)
- Culturally sensitive to Gulf customs
- Maintains elegance in different script
- Honors traditional values while being contemporary

## Key Luxury Vocabulary

**English → Arabic:**
- Timeless → خالد (khalid)
- Elegant → أنيق (aniq)
- Exclusive → حصري (hasri)
- Heritage → تراث (turath)
- Craftsmanship → حرفية (hirfiyya)
- Luxury → فخامة (fakhamah)
- Refined → راقي (raqi)
- Opulent → فاخر (fakher)
- Pristine → نقي (naqi)
- Exquisite → رائع (ra'i')

## Translation Workflow

### 1. Content Analysis

Before translating, analyze:
- Brand voice and tone
- Target audience (Gulf high-net-worth individuals)
- Cultural context
- Emotional intent
- Call-to-action effectiveness

### 2. First Draft

Create initial translation focusing on:
- Accurate meaning
- Appropriate tone
- Cultural adaptation
- Natural flow in target language

### 3. Review and Refinement

Check for:
- Luxury tone maintained
- Cultural appropriateness
- Brand consistency
- Grammar and spelling
- Readability

### 4. Native Review

Have native speaker review for:
- Natural language use
- Regional dialect appropriateness
- Cultural sensitivity
- Emotional resonance

### 5. Final QA

Verify:
- No cultural missteps
- Consistent terminology
- Proper RTL formatting
- All placeholders work correctly

## Cultural Considerations

### Gulf Market Specifics

**Do:**
- Use formal Arabic (Modern Standard Arabic)
- Reference local customs appropriately
- Consider religious sensitivities
- Emphasize family values
- Highlight exclusivity and prestige

**Don't:**
- Use overly casual language
- Make cultural assumptions
- Include inappropriate imagery descriptions
- Use idioms that don't translate

### Imagery and Visual Content

When describing products in Arabic market:
- Emphasize craftsmanship and quality
- Focus on heritage and authenticity
- Highlight exclusivity
- Use culturally appropriate metaphors

## Quality Assurance Checklist

- [ ] Luxury tone consistent across languages
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity verified
- [ ] All placeholders tested (dates, prices, names)
- [ ] No literal translations of idioms
- [ ] Brand terminology consistent
- [ ] Native speaker approved
- [ ] RTL display checked
- [ ] Length appropriate for UI
- [ ] SEO keywords included
- [ ] Call-to-action clear

## Examples

### Before (Literal Translation)
"Our watches are made with the best materials"
"ساعاتنا مصنوعة بأفضل المواد"

"Each timepiece embodies uncompromising excellence"
"كل ساعة تجسد التميز الذي لا يقبل المساومة"
